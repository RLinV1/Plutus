# Apple (AAPL) — company profile (plain English)

Educational information, not investment advice.

## What Apple does
Apple designs and sells consumer technology. Its best-known product is the
iPhone, but it also makes Mac computers, iPads, the Apple Watch, and AirPods.
Increasingly, Apple also earns money from **services** — the App Store, iCloud
storage, Apple Music, Apple TV+, and payments — which tend to bring in steady,
recurring revenue.

## Why people pay attention to Apple
- It is one of the largest companies in the world by market cap.
- It has a famously loyal customer base and a tightly integrated "ecosystem":
  once you own an iPhone, a Mac, AirPods, and an Apple Watch tend to work
  smoothly together, which keeps customers coming back.
- Its services business has been growing and carries high profit margins.

## How the business makes money
Most revenue still comes from selling hardware, with the iPhone the single
biggest contributor. The services segment is the fast-growing piece many
investors watch closely because it can smooth out the ups and downs of phone
sales.

## The big picture
Apple is often viewed as a large, established "blue chip" technology company
rather than a small, fast-growing startup. That tends to mean it is widely owned
and closely followed. As always, being well-known does not make a stock a good or
bad fit for any particular person — that depends on individual circumstances.
