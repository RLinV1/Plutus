# Microsoft (MSFT) — company profile (plain English)

Educational information, not investment advice.

## What Microsoft does
Microsoft is a giant software-and-cloud company. You probably know it for Windows
(the operating system) and Office/Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, Outlook, Teams).
But a huge part of its business today is **Azure**, its cloud-computing platform
that rents out computing power and storage to other companies over the internet.

## Why people pay attention to Microsoft
- It earns money from many different products, so it is not dependent on any
  single one — consumer software, business software, cloud, gaming (Xbox), and
  the professional network LinkedIn.
- A lot of its revenue is **subscription-based** (companies pay every month or
  year), which tends to be steady and predictable.
- It is one of the biggest players in **artificial intelligence**, partly through
  its partnership with and investment in OpenAI, and it builds AI features into
  its products.

## How the business makes money
Three broad areas: productivity software (Microsoft 365, LinkedIn), the
"intelligent cloud" (Azure and server products), and personal computing (Windows,
devices, Xbox). The cloud segment is the one investors watch most closely because
it has been the main growth engine.

## The big picture
Like Apple, Microsoft is usually seen as a large, established technology company
that is widely owned and closely followed. Being well-known doesn't make it right
or wrong for any individual.
