# What to watch out for as a beginner (plain English)

This is general, educational guidance — not investment advice, and not a
recommendation to buy or sell anything.

## Past performance is not a guarantee
A stock that went up a lot last year may or may not keep going up. Historical
returns describe what already happened; they do not predict the future.

## Diversification spreads risk
Putting all your money into a single stock means your outcome rides entirely on
that one company. Spreading money across many companies (or buying a broad index
fund that holds hundreds of them) reduces how much any single company can hurt
you. This is the idea behind "don't put all your eggs in one basket."

## Beware of hype
Stocks that are constantly in the news or hyped on social media can be exciting,
but excitement often pushes prices to levels disconnected from the underlying
business. Big, fast run-ups can reverse just as fast.

## Volatility cuts both ways
A "bumpy" stock that can jump 10% in your favor can also drop 10% against you.
Higher potential reward comes with higher potential loss.

## Watch costs and taxes
Frequent trading can rack up fees and taxes that quietly eat into returns. Many
beginners do better by keeping things simple and holding for the long term.

## Don't try to time the market
Reliably predicting short-term tops and bottoms is extremely hard, even for
professionals. A steady, long-term approach is usually less stressful and less
error-prone than jumping in and out.
