# Nvidia (NVDA) — company profile (plain English)

Educational information, not investment advice.

## What Nvidia does
Nvidia designs **chips** — specifically graphics processing units, or GPUs.
Originally these powered video-game graphics, and gaming is still a real business
for them. But the reason Nvidia became one of the most talked-about companies in
the world is that the same kind of chips turned out to be extremely good at the
heavy math behind **artificial intelligence**.

## Why people pay attention to Nvidia
- The data centers that train and run modern AI models rely heavily on Nvidia's
  chips, so the AI boom has driven enormous demand.
- Beyond the hardware, Nvidia built a widely used software platform (called CUDA)
  that makes its chips the default choice for many AI developers — a strong
  competitive moat.
- Its revenue and profits grew very quickly as AI spending surged.

## How the business makes money
The biggest segment by far is **data center** (the AI chips), followed by gaming.
Nvidia designs the chips but has them manufactured by outside foundries.

## The big picture
Nvidia is often described as a high-growth company tied closely to the AI trend.
That can mean rapid growth, but also a stock that moves a lot. Excitement and
expectations are a big part of its story — which is worth keeping in mind.
